Exploring Syracuse's water pathway

By Ali Harford

On an average day, 40 million gallons of water flow from Skaneateles Lake to the city of Syracuse.

The water exits the lake and enters the Skaneateles gatehouse, a small brick building next to a pizza shop. The gatehouse is nearly 80 years old, and controls the city of Syracuse’s entire water supply.



Because of the water quality, those 40 million gallons are only coarsely filtered and treated with fluoride and chlorine before entering water pipes in Syracuse homes. A gravity system feeds water from the lake down to Syracuse—Skaneateles Lake is nineteen miles southwest from the city, and 460 feet higher.

This water system is particularly vulnerable to HABs. Currently, there is no water treatment plant in place, water is only coarsely filtered and treated with chlorine, then later treated with UV.
