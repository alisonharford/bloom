2018 was the worst year yet for algal blooms in New York State

By Ali Harford

The New York State Department of Conservation has been tracking blooms since 2012, and at the time of this publication, had published data up to 2018.

Skaneateles Lake, in Onondaga County, experienced its first bloom in 2017. That year, the number of lakes with blooms in Onondaga County doubled, from four to eight. The county has nine total lakes.

Svetlaslava Todorova, a professor at Syracuse University who is developing a sensor that will help predict blooms by tracking nutrient levels, described the summer of 2017 as having “a storm from hell.” That, she believes, is what finally broke the resistance of Onondaga County lakes, specifically Skaneateles.
